Oriyn analyzes your PostHog event data and surfaces 3–5 distinct user archetypes per enrichment run. Each persona describes a real behavioral segment — who they are, what they do, and roughly how large that segment is. The goal is to ground your mental model of "the user" in observed behavior rather than assumption.

<Note>
  Personas require enrichment to have run. Enrichment, in turn, requires a connected PostHog integration. If you haven't set that up yet, see [PostHog integration](/integrations/posthog).
</Note>

## What a persona contains

Each persona includes:

- **`name`** — an archetype name that captures the behavioral identity of the segment (e.g., "Power User", "Occasional Explorer")
- **`description`** — a plain-language summary of who they are and what they typically do in your product
- **`behavioral_traits`** — a list of observable, data-grounded behaviors. These are derived from actual event sequences, not inferred from demographics
- **`size_estimate`** — an integer from 0–100 representing the estimated percentage of your user base this persona accounts for
- **`generated_at`** — the timestamp for when this persona was generated

The `size_estimate` field is a rough proportion, not a precise count. Use it to understand relative segment size, not to derive headcount.

## Retrieve personas

### API

```bash
curl https://api.oriyn.ai/products/{product_id}/personas \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <token>"
```

**Response:**

### CLI

```bash
oriyn personas --product-id <product_id>
```

Add `--json` to get the raw JSON response:

```bash
oriyn personas --product-id <product_id> --json
```

## Typical use cases

**Challenging internal assumptions.** Teams often build for an imagined primary user. Personas show you what behavioral segments actually exist — including ones you didn't design for.

**Prioritizing who to optimize for.** When a persona represents 40% of your user base, that's signal about where retention or activation work will have the most impact.

**Shaping onboarding or messaging.** If a large segment shares the same early drop-off pattern, that's a concrete brief for onboarding improvements.

Personas update each time enrichment runs. If your data or product changes significantly, re-running enrichment will reflect those shifts in the output.
