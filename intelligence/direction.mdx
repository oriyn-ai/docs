After enrichment runs, Oriyn generates 3–5 prescriptive recommendations for your product. These aren't generic best practices — each recommendation is derived from the behavioral signals found in your specific data. Direction tells you what to act on and why, without you having to ask.

## What direction contains

Each direction record includes a list of recommendations. Each recommendation has:

- **`title`** — a short, action-oriented description of what to do
- **`rationale`** — the behavioral evidence behind the recommendation, explained in plain language
- **`priority`** — `high`, `medium`, or `low`, based on the strength and prevalence of the underlying signals

The record also includes:

- **`derived_from`** — a list of the behavioral signals (patterns, persona traits, event sequences) that informed the recommendations
- **`generated_at`** — the timestamp for when these recommendations were generated

The `derived_from` field is your audit trail. If a recommendation surprises you, use it to trace back to the underlying data.

## Direction vs. experiments

Direction is unprompted. Oriyn surfaces it automatically based on what the data shows — you don't have to formulate a hypothesis first.

[Experiments](/experiments) work the other way: you bring a hypothesis and test it. The two are complementary. Direction is useful for discovering what to test; experiments are how you validate it.

<Tip>
  When a high-priority recommendation appears in your direction output, consider running an experiment on it. You'll have the behavioral rationale already — use it as the basis for your hypothesis and success criteria.
</Tip>

## Retrieve direction

### API

```bash
curl https://api.oriyn.ai/products/{product_id}/direction \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <token>"
```

### CLI

```bash
oriyn direction --product-id <product_id>
```

Add `--json` to get the raw JSON response:

```bash
oriyn direction --product-id <product_id> --json
```

## Typical use cases

**Generating a backlog of evidence-backed work.** Direction gives your team concrete, prioritized starting points that don't require someone to already have the right intuition.

**Aligning on priorities.** Because each recommendation includes its rationale and source signals, you can share it with stakeholders without needing to reconstruct the reasoning.

**Feeding into experiments.** High-priority recommendations are well-suited to become experiment hypotheses. The `rationale` field gives you the behavioral framing; the experiment gives you the validation.

Direction updates each time enrichment runs. Re-running enrichment after shipping changes will show whether the underlying signals have shifted — and whether the recommendations change as a result.
