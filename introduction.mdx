Most analytics tools tell you what already happened. Oriyn tells you what your users will do next — and what you should build because of it.

Oriyn is a behavioral intelligence platform. It connects to your existing event data, builds a structured understanding of your product and its users, and surfaces two things that analytics alone can't give you: **knowledge about your customers on demand** and **prescriptive direction for your product**.

## What Oriyn produces

**Customer knowledge on demand.** Once Oriyn has ingested your behavioral data, you can ask it anything about your users in plain language. Who are my power users? What do churned users have in common? Where do new signups drop off? Oriyn answers using AI-generated personas and behavioral patterns grounded in your real event data — not generic benchmarks or guesswork.

**Prescriptive product direction.** Oriyn doesn't stop at describing your users. It generates prioritized recommendations — called direction — based on what your behavioral data actually signals. Each recommendation includes a rationale and a priority level so your team knows where to focus next.

## How it's different

Amplitude tells you what users did. Oriyn tells you what they'll do next.

Traditional analytics surfaces metrics and funnels. Oriyn takes those same behavioral signals and synthesizes them into user archetypes, behavioral sequences, and actionable recommendations. You can even test a hypothesis — a feature idea, a pricing change, a new onboarding flow — against simulated user personas before you ship a single line of code.

## Who it's for

Oriyn is built for three audiences:

- **Product teams** who want to move from data to decisions faster, without needing a dedicated analyst.
- **Engineers** who want to integrate behavioral intelligence directly into their tools and workflows via the REST API or CLI.
- **AI agents** that need structured, queryable knowledge about product users to make autonomous decisions.

<Tip>
  If you're integrating Oriyn into an automated pipeline or an AI agent, the CLI and `/v1/query` endpoint are the fastest paths. See the [Quick Start](/quickstart) to get your first query running in minutes.
</Tip>
