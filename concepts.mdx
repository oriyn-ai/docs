Understanding how Oriyn's pieces fit together makes it easier to use effectively. This page covers each concept and how they relate to one another.

## Products

A product is the central object in Oriyn. It represents the thing your users interact with — your app, API, platform, or tool. Oriyn builds all of its intelligence around a product.

When you create a product, you provide a name, an optional description, and optional URLs. Oriyn uses these to begin understanding what the product does.

Every product tracks two status fields:

| Field | Values | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `context_status` | `idle` / `processing` / `ready` / `failed` | Whether context synthesis has completed |
| `enrichment_status` | `idle` / `processing` / `ready` / `failed` | Whether behavioral enrichment has completed |

Both start as `idle` and move through `processing` before settling at `ready` or `failed`. You can poll these fields to know when intelligence is available.

## Context

Context is Oriyn's structured understanding of what your product is. It's synthesized automatically when you create a product, using the name, description, and URLs you provide.

A product's context includes:

- **Company** — The organization behind the product
- **Product summary** — A concise description of what the product does
- **Core features** — The primary capabilities the product offers
- **Target users** — Who the product is built for
- **Value proposition** — The core problem it solves
- **Use cases** — Common ways users engage with it

Context feeds into everything else — it's how Oriyn knows what behavioral signals matter and how to interpret them.

<Info>
  Context synthesis runs automatically after you create or update a product. You don't need to trigger it manually. Check `context_status` to know when it's done.
</Info>

## Enrichment

Enrichment is the process of analyzing your PostHog behavioral data to generate intelligence. When you trigger enrichment, Oriyn reads your event data and produces three outputs: personas, patterns, and direction.

Enrichment requires a connected PostHog integration. Without behavioral data, Oriyn has nothing to analyze.

Track progress via `enrichment_status` on the product. Once it reaches `ready`, all three intelligence outputs are available.

<Note>
  Enrichment can be re-run at any time. Re-running updates your personas, patterns, and direction based on the latest data from PostHog.
</Note>

## Personas

A persona is an AI-generated user archetype derived from your real behavioral data. Personas represent distinct types of users — not invented marketing profiles, but patterns that emerged from how people actually use your product.

Each persona includes:

- **Name** — A descriptive label for the archetype (e.g., "The Builder", "The Evaluator")
- **Description** — A summary of who this user is and what they care about
- **Behavioral traits** — Specific behaviors that characterize this group
- **Size estimate** — The approximate percentage of your user base this persona represents

The `size_estimate` field lets you understand relative scale. A persona with a `size_estimate` of `18%` represents roughly 18% of your active users — useful for prioritization.

## Patterns

A pattern is a behavioral sequence derived from your real event data. Patterns describe how users actually move through your product — what they do, in what order, and how often.

Each pattern includes:

- **Title** — A short name for the sequence
- **Description** — What the pattern represents and why it matters
- **Frequency** — How often this sequence occurs in your user base
- **Significance** — Why this pattern is worth paying attention to
- **Raw sequence** — The ordered list of events that make up the pattern

Patterns are distinct from funnels. They're discovered from your data rather than defined upfront — Oriyn finds sequences you may not have known to look for.

## Direction

Direction is Oriyn's prescriptive output: prioritized recommendations grounded in behavioral evidence. Each piece of direction tells you something specific you should consider doing, and why your data suggests it.

Each direction item includes:

- **Title** — A clear statement of the recommendation
- **Rationale** — The behavioral evidence behind it
- **Priority** — `high`, `medium`, or `low` based on signal strength and potential impact
- **Derived from** — The signals (personas, patterns, events) that led to this recommendation

<Tip>
  Use `priority: high` items as inputs to your sprint planning or roadmap review. They represent the strongest signals in your current behavioral data.
</Tip>

## Experiments

An experiment lets you test a hypothesis against your user personas before you build anything. You describe a change — a feature, a flow, a pricing model — and Oriyn simulates how your users would respond.

Oriyn runs the simulation using 50 AI agents, each embodying one of your personas. The result is a verdict with supporting evidence:

| Field | Values | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `verdict` | `ship` / `revise` / `reject` | Whether to proceed, adjust, or abandon |
| `confidence` | `0` to `1` | How strongly the simulation supports the verdict |
| `summary` | string | A plain-language explanation of the result |
| `persona_breakdown` | object | How each persona responded to the hypothesis |

A `ship` verdict with high confidence means your personas responded positively and consistently. A `revise` verdict means there's signal worth building on but meaningful friction or resistance from at least one persona segment.

<Info>
  Experiments are simulations, not real user tests. They're most useful for early-stage validation and reducing the cost of building the wrong thing — not as a replacement for user research.
</Info>

## Integrations

Integrations are the data sources that power Oriyn's enrichment. Without behavioral data, Oriyn can synthesize context from your product description, but it cannot generate personas, patterns, or direction.

**PostHog** is currently the supported integration. Connect it via OAuth through your product's Integrations settings. Once connected, Oriyn reads your PostHog event data during enrichment.

Additional integrations are planned. See [Integrations](/integrations/overview) for current status and connection instructions.
